10 Safest European Cities for Female Solo Travelers to Explore

Emily 31.03.2026 • Reading time: 6 min

Top 10 Safest European Cities for Female Solo Travelers

Traveling solo as a woman is one of the most empowering experiences one can undertake. Europe, with its intricate history, efficient public transportation, and diverse cultures, remains the top destination for independent explorers. However, safety is always a primary concern. Choosing the right destination can make the difference between a stressful ordeal and the adventure of a lifetime. The Top 10 Safest European Cities for Female Solo Travelers offer a blend of low crime rates, welcoming local cultures, and ease of navigation that allows you to focus on the joy of discovery.

When planning your journey, remember that even in the safest cities, common sense is your best travel companion. Whether you are looking for a bustling urban center or a quaint historic town, the following list highlights cities where you can feel confident walking alone, meeting locals, and soaking in the atmosphere without constant worry.

1. Copenhagen, Denmark

Copenhagen consistently ranks as one of the world's most livable cities. It is incredibly safe, with a culture that values equality and personal freedom. The city is bicycle-friendly and well-lit at night, making it easy to traverse. Solo travelers will find the locals friendly and generally fluent in English, which removes many barriers for first-time visitors.

2. Vienna, Austria

Vienna is synonymous with elegance, safety, and a rich cultural heritage. It is a city that feels safe even after the sun goes down. Beyond its security, the city offers an unparalleled coffee culture. If you find yourself in the city, you must check out a guide to Vienna's most iconic traditional coffee houses, where you can safely sit with a book and enjoy a slice of Sachertorte while observing the world pass by.

3. Reykjavik, Iceland

Iceland is frequently cited as the safest country in the world, and its capital, Reykjavik, reflects this status perfectly. It is a small, manageable city where the crime rate is exceptionally low. The biggest "danger" in Iceland is often the unpredictable weather, so always pack accordingly.

4. Zurich, Switzerland

Known for its efficiency and cleanliness, Zurich is a fantastic base for solo travelers. While it can be expensive, it is remarkably secure. If you want to expand your trip, the surrounding regions are equally safe, allowing you to discover the best hidden gems of the Swiss Alps for nature lovers with total peace of mind.

5. Amsterdam, Netherlands

Amsterdam is vibrant, open-minded, and highly accessible. The city is designed for pedestrians and cyclists, and the prevalence of locals on the streets at all hours creates a "safety in numbers" environment. It is a fantastic place for women to explore solo, provided they are aware of the busy tourist areas.

6. Helsinki, Finland

Helsinki is a quiet, orderly city that feels very secure. The public transport system is reliable and safe, and the locals are generally respectful of personal space. It is a perfect city for those looking for a calm, introspective trip away from the chaotic pace of larger European capitals.

7. Stockholm, Sweden

Stockholm is spread across 14 islands, connected by bridges and ferries. It is a clean, modern city where digital payments are the norm, reducing the need to carry large amounts of cash. The city's layout makes it very easy to navigate, and the public transport is exceptionally safe for women traveling alone at night.

8. Munich, Germany

Munich offers the perfect balance between a big-city feel and a small-town sense of security. It is famous for its hospitality and orderly nature. As a solo traveler, you will find it easy to navigate the city’s parks and museums, and the beer gardens are generally social, safe spaces to meet other travelers.

9. Ljubljana, Slovenia

Slovenia is one of Europe’s best-kept secrets, and its capital, Ljubljana, is a charming, green, and incredibly safe city. The pedestrian-only city center allows for relaxed wandering. It is small enough to feel like a community, making it much less intimidating for a solo traveler than larger, bustling metropolises.

10. Oslo, Norway

Oslo rounds out the list with its high quality of life and low crime rates. It is a city that embraces the outdoors, and you will find that safety is woven into the social fabric. Whether you are visiting the museums or heading to the nearby fjords, you will find the environment welcoming and secure.

Essential Tips for Solo Female Travelers

  • Keep your documents secure: Always have a digital copy of your passport and insurance.
  • Stay connected: Use a local SIM card or an eSim to ensure you have GPS access at all times.
  • Trust your intuition: If a situation or a neighborhood feels "off," leave immediately.
  • Research your stay: Read recent reviews of hostels or hotels to ensure they have good security ratings.
  • Dress for the culture: Blending in helps you avoid unwanted attention.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to travel alone in Europe as a woman?
Yes, Europe is generally considered one of the safest regions in the world for solo female travelers, especially in Northern and Western European cities.
What should I do if I feel unsafe?
Trust your gut. Head toward a crowded area, enter a shop or hotel to ask for help, or call the local emergency services (112 is the universal number in the EU).
Are there any cities to avoid?
No city is 100% safe. Always research specific neighborhoods in the cities you plan to visit, as some areas may have higher petty crime rates than others.
